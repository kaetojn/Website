About ICANA

ICANA supports African growth via facilitating a network of resourceful individuals/organizations possessing genuine interests in African developmental initiatives.

Team of ICANA

Diran Otegbade

Project Initiator

A graduate of Applied Science from the University of Toronto, he is passionate about the social and economic well-being of individuals & posterity. He worked at IBM for 3.5 years as a project advisor responsible for multi-million dollar contracts across multiple business units and clients in the finance, insurance, retail & tourism sectors. Steadily determined to create his own rules & become an employer of labour, he started a real estate advisory/ property management company which invests in and manages properties in the Greater Toronto Area, Canada and Lagos, Nigeria. He is responsible for initiating ICANA and facilitating the growth of the project

Adeola Onafuwa

Project Driver

Adeola Onafuwa is a passionate Nigerian who is actively involved in the socio-political development of the African society via direct participation in non-governmental growth and developmental initiatives. He busies himself with facilitating a network of resourceful individuals/organizations that share genuine interests in African developmental initiatives. He has worked as a campaign strategist and a junior policy analyst. He has also worked as a political journalist and an editor. Currently, works with the Liberal Party of Canada as a Data Support Specialist. When Adeola is not busy crafting electoral strategies or mitigating liaisons between Africa-centric organizations, he is conveying information via his Leanwords initiative - which he sees as a viable tool for socio-political activism with the aim of redirecting the focus of youths towards active participation in the economic, social and political ethos of the African society. Adeola graduated from the University of Windsor with a B.A in Political Science. He also holds a certificate in Digital Journalism & a Diploma in International Relations and Developmental Studies.

Celestine Nema

Volunteer

Celestine was born in Rwanda, grew up in Kenya and has spent the past few years living in Toronto, Canada. She recently graduated from the University of Toronto with a Bachelor of Science and is currently a post graduate Public Administration student. Her keen interest in initiatives that aim to foster the development of African nations especially through education and health promotion is why she joined ICANA.

Nengi Adoki

Volunteer

Nengi Adoki is an alumni of both The University of Toronto and Sheridan College Institute where she studied, Communications, Culture and Information technology, English and performing arts and live production. Having founded Sync Afrique,---Canada's largest African cultural production in celebration of African culture---she is currently working in Toronto as an actress and producer. Nengi is also a founding member of the University of Toronto's African Alumni Association and is currently working with ICANA as a means of bringing African development.

Ife Ajayi

Volunteer

Ife Ajayi is a passionate Nigerian currently at University of Toronto Mississauga studying criminology and social legal studies as well as political science and gender studies; Ife Ajayi hopes to become a lawyer and politician in Nigeria. She organized and led with two others, the “Bring Back Our Girls” protest down town Toronto. Also an ex events coordinator of E.C.A.S.A ( Erindale College African Students Association) and Sync-Afrique Co-ordinator, Ife enjoys working with others to ensure progress in the African Community. Back home in Nigeria, She has a movement called “From Naija with Pride”, encouraging Nigerians tolearn to love Nigeria , be proud of it and to get involved with their community by pulling both human and material resources together.
